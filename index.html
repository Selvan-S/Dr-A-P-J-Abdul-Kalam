Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and Missile Man of India

Interesting Facts about Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Hardships: Jainulabudeen, Kalam's father, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. HIs ancestors were wealthy but due to business failures, the family fortunes were lost and the former President was forced to do odd jobs. He used to sell newspapers to support his family.

Failures: Kalam missed an opportunity to become a fighter pilot for the Inidan Air force. He was on the 9th spot on the list, and there were only 8 openings. The first 8 stortlisted candidates were recruited.

World Student's Day: Abdul Kalam's birthday i.e., 15th October is observed as the World Student's Day. This initiative was started by the UN in 2015. The motive behind this initiative is to promote diversity, multiculturalism, and innovativeness among students across the world.

Science Day: Kalam's vist to Switzerland on 26th May is commemorated as Science Day in the conuntry. This declaration in his honour was made by the Swiss Government after his demise.

Empowered Indian Nuclear Mission: Kalam also made significant contributions to the nuclear capabilities of India. The Pokhran 2 nuclear tests in 1998 were successful because of his technical support and knowledge.

Honours: He was honoured with doctorates from 40 universities.

Frisking at Airport: Kalam was frisked twice at JFK Airport in New York. He was checked for explosives by the airport staff. This incident was protested by India on the international level.

Died doing what he Loved: On his last day, he was delivering a lecture at IIM, Shillong. He was delivering a speech to hundreds of students when he suffered a cardiac arrest that made him fall to the floor. He always had a passion for teaching and working hard. And at the time when death captured him, he was doing what he loved.
